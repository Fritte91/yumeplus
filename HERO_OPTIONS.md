# Hero Section Copy Options

## Current Copy (Too Grand)
- **Title:** "The Crown Jewel of Rangsit."
- **Description:** "Unlimited Giant River Prawns, Imported Sashimi, and Wagyu Beef. Experience the fusion of Thai Seafood Culture and Japanese Precision."
- **Badges:** "Top Rated in Pathum Thani" / "Seafood Premium Buffet"

---

## Option 1: Simple & Direct (Food-First)

**Headline:** "Fresh sushi, grilled prawns, and all you can eat."

**Subheading:** "Come for the giant river prawns, stay for the sashimi. A buffet where the food speaks for itself."

**Badges:** "All You Can Eat" / "Fresh Daily"

**Why it works:**
- Puts food first with simple, appetizing language
- "Come for... stay for..." is conversational and inviting
- No grand claims or corporate speak
- "Food speaks for itself" is humble and confident
- Easy to read over food imagery

---

## Option 2: Warm & Inviting (Experience-Focused)

**Headline:** "Good food, good company, all you can eat."

**Subheading:** "Giant river prawns grilled to order, fresh sashimi, and a table full of favorites. Bring your appetite."

**Badges:** "Buffet" / "Open Daily"

**Why it works:**
- "Good food, good company" feels like a friend's recommendation
- "Bring your appetite" is warm and playful
- Focuses on the experience, not awards or titles
- Natural, conversational tone
- Appeals to groups and families

---

## Option 3: Welcoming & Casual (Restaurant-First)

**Headline:** "All you can eat sushi & seafood in Rangsit."

**Subheading:** "Fresh sashimi, grilled river prawns, and everything in between. We're here to feed you well."

**Badges:** "Sushi & Seafood" / "Buffet"

**Why it works:**
- Location-specific but not grandiose
- "Everything in between" is casual and inclusive
- "We're here to feed you well" is warm and service-oriented
- No hype, just honest restaurant language
- Feels like a neighborhood spot worth visiting

---

## Recommended Implementation: Option 1

Option 1 is recommended because it:
- Is the most food-focused
- Uses the simplest, most readable language
- Works best over rich food imagery
- Balances confidence with humility
- Appeals to all audiences (families, couples, groups)
